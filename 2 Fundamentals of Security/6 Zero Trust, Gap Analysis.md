# Zero Trust 
- Cybersec experts configure these networks with strong external defenses by using things like firewalls, intrusion protection systems, & other perimeter defenses 
- protect the systems & data using multiple levels of encryption, secure protocols, data-level authentication, & other host-base protection mechanisms 
- move to cloud when combined with rise of work from home has rapidly increased our ability to conduct secure operations within a deperimeterization architecture 
- Zero Trust demands verification for every device, user, transaction within the network, REGARDLESS of Origin 
## Control Plane
- lays out the policies  & procedures
- overarching framework & set of components responsible for defining, managing, enforcing policies related to user and system access within a organization 

1. Adaptive Identity 
2. Threat Scope Reduction 
3. Policy-driven Access Control 
4. Secured Zones 
### Adaptive Identity 
- use these that rely on real-time validation that takes into account the user's behavior, device, location, more 
### Threat Scope Reduction 
- limit users' access to only what they need for their work tasks
	- drastically reduces the network's potential attack surface 
### Policy Driven Access Control 
- entails developing, managing, enforcing user access policies based on their roles & responsibilities 
### Secured Zones 
- Isolated environments within a network that's designed to house sensitive data 
### Policy Engine 
- cross-reference the access request with its predefined policies 
- like a rulebook 
### Policy Administrator 
- used to establish & manage the access policies 


## Data Plane 
- ensures these policies are properly executed 
### Subject/System
- refers to the individual or entity attempting to gain access 
### Policy Enforcement Point 
- allow / restrict access, and it will effectively act as a gatekeeper to the sensitive areas of the systems or networks

# Gap Analysis 
- process of evaluating the differences between an orgs current performance and its desired performance 
- powerful tool that helps orgs improve their security & their performance by identifying areas where improvements can be made 
## 1 Define the Scope of the Analysis 
- identifying specific areas to be evaluated 
- the desired outcome of the analysis 
## 2 Gather Data on the Current State of the Org 
- surveys 
- interviews etc
## 3 Analyze the Data to Identify the Gaps 
- analyze where the current state of the org falls short of our desired outcomes 
- identify these gaps 
## 4 Develop a Plan to Bridge these Gaps 
- changes to processes
- changes to systems etc
- Plan should include SPECIFIC GOALS & OBJECTIVES 
- Plan should include a Timeline for achieving our goals & objectives to fill these gaps 
# Technical Gap Analysis
- involves evaluating an orgs current technical infrastructure & identifying any areas where it falls short of the technical capabilities required to fully utilize their security solutions 
# Business Gap Analysis 
- involves evaluating a orgs current business processes & identifying any areas where they fall short of the capabilities required to fully utilize cloud-based solutions 
# Plan of Action & Milestone (POA&M)
- outlines the specific measures to address each vulnerability, allocate resources, set up timelines for each remediation task that's needed
