# Security Control Categories 
### Technical Controls 
- technologies, hardware, and software mechanisms that are implemented to manage & reduce risks 
- Firewalls 
- Encryption Processes 
- Intrusion Detection Systems 
### Managerial Controls 
- involve the strategic planning of governance side of security 
- beyond risk assessments, managerial controls also encompass security policies, training programs, incident response strategies 
### Operational Controls
- procedures & measures that's designed to protect data on a day-to-day basis & are mainly governed by internal processes & human actions 
- Backup Procedures 
- Account Reviews 
- User Training Programs 
### Physical Controls 
- tangible, real-world measures taken to protect assets 
- Shredding Sensitive Documents 
- Security Guards 
- Locking the doors 
- about appreciating the multifaceted layers of protection that come together to form a robust security posture

# Security Control Types 
### Preventative Controls 
- proactive measures implemented to thwart potential security threats or breaches 
### Deterrent Controls 
- aim to discourage potential attackers by making the effort seem less appealing or more challenging 
### Detective Controls 
- monitor & alert orgs to malicious activities as they occur or shortly thereafter 
- IDS: Intrusion Detection System 
### Corrective Controls 
- mitigate any potential damage & restore the systems to their normal state 
### Compensating Controls 
- alternative measures that are implemented when primary security controls aren't feasible or effective 
### Directive Controls 
- often rooted in policy or documentation & set the standards for behavior within a org 
- Orgs AUP (Acceptable Use Policy) is considered a directive control
