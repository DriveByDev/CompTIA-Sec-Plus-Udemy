# Frauds & Scams OBJ 2.2
## Fraud
- wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain 
## Identity Fraud
- use by one person of another person's personal info, w/o auth, to commit a crime or deceive/defraud that other person or a third person 
## Scam
- fraudulent or deceptive act or operation 
## Invoice Scam
- in which person is tricked into paying for fake invoice for a service/product that they didn't order

# Influence Campaigns OBJ 2.2
- coordinated efforts to affect public perception or behavior towards a particular cause, individual, or group 
## Misinformation
- inaccurate information shared unintentionally 
## Disinformation
- intentional spread of false info to deceive or mislead

# Other Social Engineering Attacks OBJ 2.2
## Diversion Theft
- manipulating a situation or creating distraction to steal valuable items or info 
## Hoax
- malicious deception that's often spread through social media, email, other communication channels 
## Shoulder Surfing
- looking over someone's shoulder to gather personal info 
## Dumpster Diving 
- searching through trash to find valuable info 
## Eavesdropping
- process of secretly listening to private conversations 
## Baiting 
- planting malware-infected device for a victim to find & unintentionally introduce malware to their orgs system 
## Piggybacking & Tailgating 
- both involve unauth person following an auth person into a secure area 
## Tailgating
- attacker attempts to follow employee through an access control vestibule or access control point w/o their knowledge 
## Piggybacking 
- attacker tricks an auth employee into using their access badge to grant them entry into the facility
