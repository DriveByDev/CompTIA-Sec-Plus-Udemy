# Unskilled Attackers 
- an individual who lacks the technical knowledge to develop their own hacking tools or exploits
- they can still cause significant damage using readily available tools & exploits to victimize systems with unpatched, known vulnerabilities 
	- DDOS attacks
- many Unskilled Attackers are motivated by the desire for recognition or the thrill of causing disruption to an orgs network 
- less likely to be motivated by financial gain or political ideologies 
- can usually kick an individual workstation off the network w/o much effort 
# Hacktivists
- individuals or groups that use their technical skills to promote a cause or drive social change instead of for personal gain 
	- Website Defacement (Digital Graffiti)
	- DDOS Attacks (Distributed Denial of Service) 
	- Doxing (Releasing Private Information to the Public)
	- Leaking of Sensitive Data 
- tend to demonstrate a fairly high level of sophistication 
- primarily motivated by their ideological beliefs rather than trying to achieve financial gains 
- target orgs or individuals they perceive as acting out against their cause 
# Organized Crime
- sophisticated and well-structured entities that leverage resources & technical skills for illicit gain 
	- Custom Malware 
	- Ransomware
	- Sophisticated Phishing Campaigns
- demonstrate high level of adaptability due to constantly evolving their methods in response to new security measures & opportunities 
- known to exploit emerging technologies 
	- cryptocurrencies 
	- dark web
	- cellular collection devices 
- main motivation is financial gain
	- Data breaches 
	- Identity Theft 
	- Online Fraud 
	- Ransomware Attacks 
# Nation-State Actors
- groups that are sponsored by a government to conduct cyber operations against other nations, orgs, or individuals 
**False Flag Attack**
- attack that's orchestrated in such a way that it appears to originate from a different source or group 
	- Creating Custom Malware 
	- Using Zero-day Exploits 
	- Becoming an Advanced Persistent Threat 

			Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)
	- term used to be used synonymously with a nation-state actor because of their long-term persistence and stealth 

- funded by governments to conduct cyber operations that can help their governments achieve political objectives 
	- Gathering intelligence 
	- Disrupting Critical Infrastructure 
	- Influencing Political Processes
	- Espionage 
	- North Korea
